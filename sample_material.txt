# Cloud Computing Fundamentals
Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence—over the Internet (“the cloud”) to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

## Key Concepts
1. **Scalability**: The ability to increase or decrease resources based on demand.
2. **Elasticity**: The ability to automatically scale resources in real-time.
3. **PaaS (Platform as a Service)**: A cloud computing model where a third-party provider delivers hardware and software tools to users over the internet.
4. **IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)**: High-level APIs used to dereference various low-level details of underlying network infrastructure.
5. **SaaS (Software as a Service)**: Software that is deployed over the internet and is centrally hosted.
